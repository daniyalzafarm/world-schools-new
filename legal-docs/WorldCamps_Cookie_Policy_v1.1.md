## 1. Introduction and Scope

**1.1** This Cookie Policy ("**Policy**") explains how World Schools Sàrl ("**World Camps**", "**we**", "**us**", "**our**") uses cookies, local storage, pixels, and other similar tracking and storage technologies on the World Camps platform ("**Platform**").

**1.2** This Policy supplements the World Camps [Privacy Policy] and forms part of the legal framework governing your use of the Platform together with the [Customer Terms and Conditions] and the [Provider Terms and Conditions]. Capitalised terms not defined here have the meaning given in those documents.

**1.3 Who this Policy covers.** This Policy applies to:

- Customers (parents, legal guardians, and other adults who use the Platform to discover or book Programmes);
- visitors who browse the Platform without registering;
- Provider representatives who interact with the Platform on behalf of Provider organisations.

**1.4 Children and Minors.** The Platform is contracted with adult Customers, not directly with Children or Minors. We do not knowingly serve advertising cookies or behavioural-tracking technologies to sessions associated with an identified Minor. Specific safeguards are set out in Section 7.

**1.5 Jurisdiction.** World Schools Sàrl is incorporated in Switzerland. This Policy is designed to comply with the Swiss Federal Act on Data Protection ("**FADP**"), the EU General Data Protection Regulation ("**GDPR**") and the ePrivacy Directive (2002/58/EC), the UK GDPR and the Privacy and Electronic Communications Regulations 2003 ("**PECR**"), the US Children's Online Privacy Protection Act ("**COPPA**"), and applicable international data protection and electronic communications standards. **1.6 Self-hosted consent infrastructure.** Our cookie consent banner and Cookie Preference Centre are built and operated by World Camps. ## 2. Definitions

**"Cookie"** means a small text file placed on your device by a website or application, which may be used to store information about your visit and to recognise your device on subsequent visits.

**"Cookie Preference Centre"** means the in-Platform interface that allows you to view, set, and update your cookie preferences by category.

**"Essential Cookie"** means a Cookie or similar technology that is strictly necessary to provide a service you have requested, including authentication, security, fraud prevention, load balancing, and basic functionality of the Platform.

**"Local Storage"** means browser-based persistent storage mechanisms (including HTML5 localStorage and sessionStorage) that store information on your device outside of traditional Cookies.

**"Non-essential Cookie"** means any Cookie or similar technology that is not an Essential Cookie, including functional, analytics, marketing, and personalisation Cookies.

**"Pixel"** means a small image, beacon, or script embedded in a webpage or email that enables the recording of certain user actions or device characteristics.

**"Storage Technology"** means any client-side storage mechanism, including Cookies, Local Storage, IndexedDB, and similar technologies.

**"Tracker"** means any technology used to collect information about your interactions with the Platform, including Cookies, Pixels, Local Storage, scripts, and software development kits ("SDKs").

Other capitalised terms (including "**Booking**", "**Child**", "**Customer**", "**Minor**", "**Personal Data**", "**Platform**", "**Provider**", and "**User Account**") have the meaning given in the Privacy Policy and the Customer Terms and Conditions.

## 3. How and Why We Use Cookies and Similar Technologies

**3.1 Purposes.** We use Cookies and other Storage Technologies to:

- operate, secure, and maintain the Platform, including authenticating Users and preventing fraud or abuse;
- remember your preferences, including language, region, and currency;
- enable booking, payment, and messaging functionality;
- understand how the Platform is used and improve its performance and content;
- (with your consent) personalise Programme recommendations and other content; and
- (with your consent) measure the effectiveness of our marketing activities.

**3.2 Legal basis.** Where Cookies or similar technologies process Personal Data, the applicable legal basis is:

- for Essential Cookies, our legitimate interest in operating a secure and functional Platform and, where applicable, performance of the contract with you;
- for Non-essential Cookies, your consent freely given through the Cookie Preference Centre, which may be withdrawn at any time.

**3.3 Consent gating.** Non-essential Cookies and Trackers do not load until you have provided consent to the relevant category through the consent banner or the Cookie Preference Centre. Rejecting a category prevents the corresponding Cookies and Trackers from being set or executed.

**3.4 Storage Technologies treated as equivalent to Cookies.** Where the law applicable to your session treats Local Storage, Pixels, or other Storage Technologies in the same way as Cookies, we apply the same consent and disclosure standards described in this Policy to those technologies.

## 4. Categories of Cookies and Trackers We Use

We classify the Cookies and Trackers used on the Platform into the following categories. Each category corresponds to a toggle in the Cookie Preference Centre, except for Strictly Necessary, which is always on and cannot be disabled.

### 4.1 Strictly Necessary

Cookies and Storage Technologies in this category are essential to operate the Platform. They cannot be disabled. They include authentication and session management, security and fraud prevention, load balancing, basic site functionality, and payment processing through our payment processor.

### 4.2 Functional and Preference

Cookies and Storage Technologies in this category remember your choices and improve your experience. They include language, currency, region, accessibility, and onboarding progress preferences. They are not loaded until you accept this category.

### 4.3 Analytics

Cookies and Storage Technologies in this category help us understand how Users interact with the Platform, on an aggregated and de-identified basis where reasonably possible, in order to improve performance, identify defects, and prioritise development. They are not loaded until you accept this category.

### 4.4 Marketing and Advertising

Cookies, Pixels, and Storage Technologies in this category measure the performance of our marketing activities and support advertising on third-party platforms. They may be set by our marketing partners on our instruction. They are not loaded until you accept this category, and they are never set in respect of sessions identified as belonging to a Minor.

### 4.5 AI and Personalisation

Cookies and Storage Technologies in this category support personalised Programme recommendations, the AI-assisted knowledge base, and related AI-driven Platform features. They are not loaded until you accept this category. This category is also surfaced in your account settings under "AI personalisation" so that you can adjust it without leaving your account.

### 4.6 Embedded Third-Party Content

Where the Platform embeds content hosted by third parties (for example, maps, video players, fonts loaded from third-party content delivery networks, or social-media widgets), those third parties may set their own Cookies or Storage Technologies. Embedded third-party content is loaded only after you accept the category that corresponds to the function of the embed (typically Functional or Marketing). Where the embedded content is essential to a function you have requested (for example, a map you have explicitly opened), it loads under the Strictly Necessary category for the duration of that interaction.

## 7. Children's Privacy and Cookies

**7.1 No advertising or behavioural tracking of Minors.** We do not set Marketing or Advertising Cookies, or apply behavioural-tracking technologies, to sessions that we have identified as belonging to a Minor. Where a session is associated with an identified Minor (including through a Customer's Child profile context), Non-essential categories remain disabled regardless of any consent given at the device level.

**7.3 No knowing collection from under-13s in the United States.** Consistent with the Privacy Policy COPPA supplement, we do not knowingly use Cookies or Trackers to collect Personal Data directly from children under 13 in the United States.

## 8. Managing Your Cookie Preferences

### 8.1 Consent banner and Cookie Preference Centre

**8.1.1** When you first access the Platform, a consent banner is presented offering three options of equal visual prominence: **Accept All**, **Reject All**, and **Manage Preferences**. Rejecting all Non-essential categories does not impair access to the Platform or to any service that does not depend on a Non-essential Cookie.

**8.1.2** The Cookie Preference Centre allows you to set granular preferences for each category described in Section 4. Strictly Necessary cannot be disabled. All other categories default to off and require an affirmative opt-in.

**8.1.3** A persistent "Cookie Preferences" link is available in the footer of every page of the Platform. Selecting that link opens the Cookie Preference Centre and allows you to change your preferences at any time.

### 8.2 Browser controls and Do-Not-Track signals

**8.2.1** Most web browsers allow you to control Cookies through their settings, including by blocking Cookies, deleting Cookies, or being notified when a Cookie is set. Disabling Cookies through your browser may impair the Platform's functionality.

**8.2.2** Where your browser sends a Global Privacy Control ("GPC") or equivalent opt-out signal that we are required to recognise under applicable law, we treat that signal as a rejection of Non-essential categories for the relevant session.

### 8.3 Withdrawing consent

**8.3.1** You may withdraw consent to any Non-essential category at any time through the Cookie Preference Centre. Withdrawal takes effect from the time it is recorded and does not affect the lawfulness of processing based on consent before its withdrawal.

**8.3.2** Once a category is disabled, the corresponding Cookies and Trackers cease to be set. Cookies already stored on your device that fall within the disabled category are deleted or expired in accordance with our cookie management process. You may also delete Cookies manually through your browser.

### 8.4 Re-prompt

**8.4.1** We may re-prompt you for cookie consent following a material change to the cookie inventory, on expiry of the consent record, or where required by applicable law.

The following supplements apply in addition to the main body of this Policy for users in the specified jurisdictions. In the event of conflict between a supplement and the main Policy, the supplement prevails for users in the relevant jurisdiction.

### 11.1 European Union — GDPR and ePrivacy Supplement

**Legal framework.** Use of Cookies and similar technologies in respect of EU Users is governed by Article 5(3) of the ePrivacy Directive (2002/58/EC, as amended) as transposed in the relevant Member State, and, where Cookies involve the processing of Personal Data, by the GDPR.

**Consent standard.** Consent for Non-essential Cookies is collected in accordance with Article 4(11) and Article 7 GDPR and the European Data Protection Board Guidelines 05/2020. Consent is freely given, specific, informed, and unambiguous, given by a clear affirmative action, and may be withdrawn as easily as it is given.

**No cookie walls.** Access to the Platform is not conditional on consent to Non-essential Cookies.

**International transfers.** Transfers of Personal Data outside the EEA arising from Cookies and Trackers are conducted under Standard Contractual Clauses or other lawful transfer mechanisms, as described in Section 6 of the Privacy Policy.

**Supervisory authority.** EU Users have the right to lodge complaints with their national supervisory authority. World Schools Sàrl is established in Switzerland and does not have a main establishment in the EU; the GDPR "one-stop-shop" lead-supervisory-authority mechanism therefore does not apply, and each national supervisory authority within the EU may exercise its competence directly in respect of data subjects resident in its territory.

---

### 11.2 United Kingdom — UK GDPR, PECR and AADC Supplement

**Legal framework.** Use of Cookies and similar technologies in respect of UK Users is governed by Regulation 6 of PECR and, where Personal Data is involved, by the UK GDPR and the Data Protection Act 2018.

**Consent standard.** PECR-compliant consent is collected through the consent banner and Cookie Preference Centre. Strictly Necessary Cookies are loaded without consent in reliance on the PECR Regulation 6(4) exemption.

**Age Appropriate Design Code.** Where the Platform is accessible to users in the United Kingdom, we apply the standards of the ICO's Age Appropriate Design Code, including high-privacy defaults for child-facing features, no geolocation tracking of children, no nudge techniques in cookie-related interfaces directed at or reachable by children, and no profiling of children for marketing purposes.

**International transfers.** Transfers of Personal Data from the UK arising from Cookies and Trackers are conducted under the UK International Data Transfer Agreement, or an addendum to EU SCCs, as appropriate.

**Supervisory authority.** UK Users may lodge complaints with the Information Commissioner's Office (ICO): www.ico.org.uk.

---

### 11.3 United States — COPPA, CCPA/CPRA and State Privacy Supplement

**COPPA.** Consistent with Section 17.3 of the Privacy Policy, we do not knowingly use Cookies or Trackers to collect personal information from children under 13 in the United States. The Platform is designed for use by parents and guardians.

**Behavioural advertising to under-13s.** We do not use personal information of children under 13 for targeted advertising or behavioural profiling.

**CCPA / CPRA and other US state privacy laws.** Where you are a resident of California or another US state whose privacy law applies, you have the right to opt out of the "sale" or "sharing" of personal information for cross-context behavioural advertising (or the analogous concept under your state's law). You may exercise this right through the Cookie Preference Centre or by enabling the Global Privacy Control signal in your browser. We honour validated GPC signals as an opt-out of sale and sharing as required by applicable law.

**Disclosures.** California Users may request the categories of personal information collected through Cookies and Trackers, and the categories of third parties to whom such information is disclosed, by contacting privacy@world-camps.org.

**Applicable law.** World Camps is incorporated in Switzerland. Our use of Cookies and similar technologies is carried out in accordance with the revised FADP (in force 1 September 2023) and the principles of transparency, proportionality, and purpose limitation.

**Consent.** Where Cookies process Personal Data and consent is required under the FADP or applicable EEA standards relevant to the relevant session, consent is collected through the Cookie Preference Centre on the same basis as set out in Section 8.

**Cross-border transfers.** Transfers of Personal Data from Switzerland arising from Cookies and Trackers are conducted in accordance with the FADP's transfer requirements, including contractual safeguards where the recipient country does not offer an adequate level of protection.

**Supervisory authority.** Swiss residents may lodge complaints with the Federal Data Protection and Information Commissioner (FDPIC): www.edoeb.admin.ch.
